About Me

I am Prince Annobil Coleman, a results-oriented professional who bridges the worlds of public health, data science, and human-centred service. Born in Ghana and now based in London, I bring a truly international perspective to every challenge I take on.

My career spans retail, education, healthcare, and NGO sectors across Ghana and the UK. I have worked with Nestlé Ghana managing commercial client relationships, taught primary school children, supported hospital social work units, and most recently, I completed a demanding MSc programme while working full-time — a testament to my drive and discipline.

My dissertation investigated barriers and facilitators of healthcare access for BAME immigrant communities — combining qualitative research with data-driven analysis to produce actionable insights for policy and practice. I am fluent in English and Twi, and I am passionate about using data to drive social impact.
